# Starbase Omega – Operations and Personnel Policy 2157

## 1. About Starbase Omega

Starbase Omega is a deep-space research station located at coordinates 47.3N, 221.8E in the Kepler-22 system.
The station has 312 crew members divided into four divisions:

- Division of Quantum Research (DQR) – 98 crew
- Division of Life Support Engineering (DLS) – 87 crew
- Division of Xenobiology (DXB) – 74 crew
- Division of Security and Defense (DSD) – 53 crew

The station orbits Kepler-22b at an altitude of 4,200 km and completes one orbit every 31 hours.
Starbase Omega was commissioned on March 14, 2141 and has been continuously operational for 16 years.
The station commander is Admiral Yara Osei, appointed in 2154 after the retirement of Admiral Henric Voss.
The station operates on a 26-hour day cycle synchronized with Kepler-22b surface time.

## 2. Security Clearance Levels

All information on Starbase Omega is classified into four levels:

- Level 0 – Public information, freely shareable
- Level 1 – Internal crew use only, not for external transmission
- Level 2 – Classified research data, requires divisional head approval to access
- Level 3 – Restricted — requires dual authorization from two division heads and a 72-hour waiting period

Access to Level 3 data requires biometric verification including retinal scan, voice print, and DNA sample.
Level 3 clearance is currently held by only 7 crew members on the station.
All data transfers above Level 1 are logged and reviewed weekly by the DSD.

## 3. Approved Research Activities

The following research activities are approved on Starbase Omega:

- Quantum entanglement experiments below 4.2 terajoules
- Xenobiological sample analysis in containment lab B-7
- Atmospheric modeling of Kepler-22b surface conditions
- Cryogenic storage of biological specimens below -196°C
- Dark matter mapping using the station's array of 24 gravitational sensors
- Subspace communication research within frequency band 7.4 to 9.1 GHz
- Geological core analysis of samples retrieved from Kepler-22b surface missions

The following activities are strictly prohibited without explicit Level 3 authorization:

- Uncontrolled antimatter reactions of any scale
- Direct contact with unquarantined xenobiological specimens
- Unauthorized use of the quantum drive for personal travel
- Any experiment exceeding the 4.2 terajoule threshold
- Attempts to communicate with the unidentified signal source detected in sector 9
- Modifications to the life support system without DLS supervisor approval

## 4. Station Rules

1. Airlocks may only be operated by certified crew members with DSD clearance.
2. The quantum drive may not be activated within 500 km of the station.
3. All crew members must complete a 14-day quarantine period after returning from planetary surface missions.
4. Personal use of the replicator is limited to 3 meals per day per crew member.
5. Emergency drills are conducted every 15 days at 06:00 station time.
6. Crew members are not permitted in the observation deck during solar flare events.
7. All personal communication with Earth must be logged and may be subject to review.
8. The recreational area on deck 12 is available between 18:00 and 23:00 station time.
9. Pets are not permitted on the station with the exception of approved emotional support animals.
10. All crew members must wear identification badges at all times when outside their quarters.

## 5. Medical Policy

All crew members must undergo a full medical scan every 30 days in the medical bay on deck 4.
Crew members showing symptoms of Kepler Syndrome must report to the medical bay within 2 hours of symptom onset.

Kepler Syndrome symptoms include:
- Disorientation and confusion lasting more than 10 minutes
- Blue skin discoloration, particularly around the fingernails and lips
- Temporary loss of short-term memory
- Sensitivity to light above 800 lux
- Unexplained fatigue lasting more than 48 hours

The station carries a maximum of 90 days of medical supplies at all times.
The chief medical officer is Dr. Lena Vasquez, who has served in this role since 2149.
A resupply of medical materials is scheduled every 60 days via the automated cargo shuttle from Relay Station 7.
Crew members may not self-administer any medication classified above Category 2 without medical officer approval.

## 6. Emergency Procedures

### Hull Breach Protocol

In the event of a hull breach:

1. Activate emergency bulkheads within 30 seconds of breach detection.
2. All non-essential crew must proceed to emergency pods on deck 7.
3. The station commander must be notified within 60 seconds.
4. DSD personnel will seal the affected section within 5 minutes.
5. Life support will automatically reroute oxygen supply to unaffected sections.
6. Repair drones will be deployed from bay 3 within 10 minutes.

### Xenobiological Containment Failure

In the event of a xenobiological containment failure:

1. Initiate Protocol Zeta immediately by pressing the red emergency panel in any corridor.
2. Seal all access points to lab B-7 using the manual override controls.
3. All crew within 50 meters must proceed to decontamination bay 3 on deck 2.
4. Do not attempt to contain the specimen without full hazmat equipment rated for Class 4 biological hazards.
5. Contact DXB division head Dr. Tariq Mbeki immediately.
6. The station will enter full lockdown until the DSD clears the affected zone.

### Power Failure Protocol

In the event of a total power failure:

1. Emergency batteries will activate automatically and provide 72 hours of life support.
2. The DLS must restore primary power within 48 hours.
3. All non-essential systems will be shut down to conserve power.
4. The quantum research division must suspend all active experiments immediately.
5. Crew must report to their designated emergency stations within 15 minutes.

## 7. Supply and Logistics

Starbase Omega receives resupply missions every 60 days from the United Earth Space Authority.
Each resupply mission delivers approximately 40 metric tons of food, medical supplies, and equipment.
The station maintains a minimum 90-day emergency reserve of all critical supplies.
Water is recycled on-station with an efficiency rate of 97.3%, reducing dependency on resupply for water.
Fuel for the station's positioning thrusters is delivered separately every 180 days.

The station's replicator system can synthesize basic food items, clothing, and minor equipment parts.
Replicator usage is tracked per crew member and reviewed monthly by the logistics officer.
Crew members who exceed their replicator allocation three times in a month will have their allocation reduced by 20% for the following month.

## 8. Communication Policy

All external communications from Starbase Omega are routed through the relay network at Relay Station 7.
Communication with Earth has a one-way delay of approximately 620 light-seconds.
Emergency communications are given priority bandwidth and bypass the standard queue.
Personal messages to family members on Earth are limited to 500 megabytes per crew member per week.
All research data transmissions must be encrypted using the station's quantum encryption protocol before transmission.
Unauthorized use of the subspace radio array is grounds for immediate suspension of communication privileges.

## 9. Code of Conduct

All crew members of Starbase Omega are expected to maintain the highest standards of professional conduct.
Harassment, discrimination, or intimidation of any crew member will result in immediate disciplinary action.
Disputes between crew members must be reported to the division head within 24 hours.
Physical altercations are grounds for immediate removal from the station on the next available transport.
Crew members are expected to support each other during the psychological challenges of deep-space isolation.
A counseling service is available 24 hours a day through Dr. Amara Singh on deck 4, room 14.

## 10. End of Mission Protocol

When a crew member's tour of duty ends, the following steps must be completed:

1. Return all station equipment and identification badges to the logistics office.
2. Complete a full debrief with the division head lasting no less than 2 hours.
3. Undergo a final medical scan and psychological evaluation.
4. All personal research data must be reviewed and approved for export by Level 2 clearance personnel.
5. The crew member will be transported to Relay Station 7 on the next scheduled shuttle, departing every 30 days.
